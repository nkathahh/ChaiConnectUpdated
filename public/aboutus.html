Our Story

Built for the People
Who Grow Kenya's Tea

ChaiConnect started with a simple question: why does a farmer have to wait a month to find out how much their tea is worth?

The Problem We Saw

A Supply Chain Running on Paper

Kenya is the world's third-largest tea exporter. More than 600,000 smallholder farmers contribute over 60% of total tea production. Yet for decades, the day-to-day operations of this industry have run on paper delivery books, manual weighbridge registers and end-of-month payment statements that farmers often couldn't verify.

Disputes over weights, unexplained payment deductions and a total lack of visibility into quality grading were commonplace. Extension officers typed up field visit reports days after the fact. Factory managers reconciled intake records by hand each evening.

ChaiConnect was built to fix this — not with a complex enterprise system that requires a consultant to run, but with a straightforward, accessible platform that works for everyone from a 0.5-acre smallholder in Nyeri to a multi-factory administrator in Nairobi.

Our Mission

To make every transaction in Kenya's tea supply chain transparent, verifiable and accessible — so that farmers are paid fairly and on time, factories operate efficiently, and policymakers can make decisions backed by real data.

🌍

Our Vision

A tea industry in East Africa where no farmer is left in the dark about what their crop is worth, where quality improvements are incentivised with immediate feedback, and where digital tools empower every actor in the chain.

⚖️

Our Values

Transparency in every transaction. Accessibility for every user regardless of connectivity or device. Respect for the farmer as the foundation of the entire industry. Continuous improvement through community feedback.

Technology

How ChaiConnect Is Built

ChaiConnect is a web-based platform built with a Python/Flask backend, a relational database for all transaction records, and a responsive HTML/CSS/JS frontend that works on any device. The USSD layer is integrated via Africa's Talking API, and SMS notifications are sent through the same gateway.

All data is stored securely with role-based access controls enforced at both the application and database level. The platform is hosted on a cloud infrastructure with automated daily backups.
